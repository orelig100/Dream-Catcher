Dream Catcher

We're building a wearable tFUS-based system to induce lucid dreams and help treat PTSD nightmares. Our tech activates the brain precisely during REM sleep.

About Us

Dream Catcher is a multidisciplinary team aiming to revolutionize sleep and trauma therapy using non-invasive focused ultrasound. We combine neuroscience, engineering, and innovation to help users regain control over their dreams.

How It Works

  1. EEG Monitoring: Detects REM sleep in real-time.
  2. Nightmare Detection: Identifies stress patterns using AI.
  3. tFUS Activation: Applies focused ultrasound to trigger lucid awareness.
  4. Lucid Intervention: The dreamer takes control and exits the nightmare.

Nightmare Prevalence and PTSD

  • Levrier et al. (2016). Nightmare frequency, nightmare distress and the efficiency of trauma-focused cognitive behavioral therapy for PTSD. Archives of Trauma Research, 5(3). – Nightmare prevalence among PTSD sufferers.
  • McWhorter et al. (2017). A wearable health monitoring system for PTSD. Biol. Inspired Cogn. Arch., 22, 44–50. – Background on PTSD nightmare detection systems.
  • Tanev et al. (2017). Nightmares and heart rate response in PTSD. J. Nerv. Ment. Dis., 205(4), 308–312. – Parasympathetic dysfunction in PTSD nightmares.

Physiological Data and Wearables

  • Walch (2019). Motion and heart rate from a wrist-worn wearable. PhysioNet. – Physiological signal dataset.
  • Goldberger et al. (2000). PhysioBank, PhysioToolkit, and PhysioNet. Circulation, 101(23), e215–e220. – Core reference for physiological data processing.
  • Wang et al. (n.d.). Multi-modal flexible headband for sleep monitoring. [Unpublished manuscript] – Wearable for sleep data acquisition.

tFUS and Stimulation Protocols

Lucid Dreaming and Brain Activation
